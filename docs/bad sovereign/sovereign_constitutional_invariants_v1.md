---

## 2. Invariants

The following invariants SHALL hold across all `{sovereign}` specifications and implementations.

1. **Control authority invariant**  
   `{zeno}` SHALL remain the sole owner of the authoritative workflow graph.

2. **Risk-boundary invariant**  
   Risk class SHALL be assigned before consequential progression and MAY be elevated, but SHALL NOT be silently lowered by local production or scoring components.

3. **Assurance invariant**  
   Local evaluation SHALL NOT substitute for assurance when the risk class requires stronger review.

4. **Consequence-routing invariant**  
   No provisional output SHALL cross into governed communication or governed execution without passing the risk-appropriate boundary.

5. **Dispatch / execution separation invariant**  
   Communication consequence and execution consequence SHALL remain distinct, even when both emerge from the same approval state.

6. **Approval invariant**  
   Review SHALL NOT be treated as equivalent to approval.

7. **Completion / archival invariant**  
   Completion and archival SHALL remain separate acts and separate states.

8. **Provenance invariant**  
   No active governed object SHALL lose provenance.

9. **Interface clarity invariant**  
   No consequential object SHALL omit risk class, owner, and workflow linkage.

10. **Memory-governance invariant**  
    Durable memory SHALL remain governed by scope, provenance, and risk policy.

11. **Human-boundary invariant**  
    Human oversight at higher-risk edges is structural, not optional theater.

12. **Constitutional precedence invariant**  
    If a lower-level specification conflicts with a higher-level constitutional rule, the higher-level rule SHALL prevail.

---

## 3. Conclusion

These invariants are the non-negotiable constitutional spine of `{sovereign}`.
